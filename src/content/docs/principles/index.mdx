Development principles are rules and guidelines that help us write higher-quality code. These principles have been shaped by the collective experience of thousands of developers and are universal, regardless of the programming language.

---

## The 4 Fundamental Pillars

> *"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication"*
<Card title="1. KISS - Keep It Simple, Stupid">
    The KISS principle reminds us that simple solutions are often the best. Simple code is easier to understand, maintain, and debug.
</Card>

> *"The foundations of robust object-oriented code"*
<Card title="2. SOLID - The 5 OOP Principles">
    SOLID is an acronym that groups 5 essential principles for designing maintainable and extensible classes and modules.
</Card>

> *"Every piece of knowledge must have a single representation"*
<Card title="3. DRY - Don't Repeat Yourself">
    DRY encourages us to avoid duplicating code and logic, making our code more maintainable and less error-prone.
</Card>

> *"Only talk to your immediate friends"*
<Card title="4. Law of Demeter">
    This principle limits dependencies between objects, creating more modular and loosely coupled code.
</Card>
